About Me

I am currently working as a GIS and Reomte Sensing Lab Technician at Chinhoyi University of Technology. I received my Honours Geography degree in Human Environment from the University of Zimbabwe. Throughout my college career, I studied GIS, Remote Sensing and Spatial analysis. For my dissertation, I specialized in spatial data infrastructure and designed a geoportal prototype. Link: Snaps Geoportal. I worked for Scientific and Industrial Research and Development Center (SIRDC) in Harare under the Geo-information and Remote Sensing Institute (GRSI) as an intern. During that time, I participated in Agricultural Mechanization Projects, Geo-database designing, Environmental Impact Assessment, Cadaster mapping including creating subsequent spatial database, GIS trainings and was awarded a Certificate in GIS and Remote Sensing.

I enjoy using GIS to solve complex problems and to gain a greater understanding of our environment and society. Although much of my experience with GIS has been related to spatial data infrastructure and natural resource management, I have also applied my spatial analysis experience toward public health-related research and the mapping of the urban environment. I have worked with large amounts of data - organizing, sorting, and summarizing existing datasets, and creating databases of my own. I pay close attention to detail, and I enjoy discovering new analysis methods and techniques.

I have gained a good deal of supervisory experience, as I have directly coordinated and supervised seasonal employees, and student research groups. I have also collaborated with organizations, such as Ministry of Energy and Power Development and World Vision. I greatly enjoy teaching and sharing my knowledge of GIS, Remote Sensing and computer programming.
